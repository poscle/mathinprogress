Our vision

Fostering a deep understanding and appreciation of mathematics, empowering students to think critically, reason logically, and apply concepts confidently in everyday problem-solving.

MathInProgress was founded by students who believe that understanding the why behind mathematics is just as important as knowing the how. Every article here is built around that same question.

Our mission
01
Make things clear. Write about mathematical concepts in a way that actually makes sense, focusing on the ideas underneath rather than just the steps.
02
Ask better questions. Look at patterns, connections, and applications across Algebra, Geometry, and beyond. Maths is more interesting when you notice how everything links up.
03
Build confidence. Create a space where it's okay to try things out, get stuck, ask questions, and figure things out on your own terms.
04
Keep it enjoyable. Mathematics shouldn't feel like a chore. We want people to come away from these articles actually interested in the subject.
🔍
The why before the how
We don't just state formulas and move on. Every article starts from the question of why something is true in the first place.
🧩
Connections over compartments
A lot of things in maths are more connected than they look. Part of what we try to do is show those connections rather than treating each topic as its own island.
✍️
Written by students
Every article is written by a student who not too long ago was confused by the same thing. That tends to make the explanations a bit more honest.

The MIP Championship

Most math competitions test whether you got the right answer. Ours won't.

The MIP Championship is a student-led initiative inspired by the International Mathematical Olympiad, the world's most prestigious math competition, known for heavy proofs and questions that have stumped even the brightest students. Unlike typical olympiads that use a correct/incorrect scoring system (and can be trained for through private coaching), we want to do something different.

Problems will be diverse, with the majority involving proofs. Marks are awarded holistically based on the student's full working, the reasoning, the approach, the ideas shown, not just whether the final answer is right. The goal is to make something engaging and genuinely fun, not just another high-pressure test.

1.
Proof-based problems that reward mathematical thinking, not just correct answers
2.
Holistic marking, your full working is assessed, including the ideas and reasoning shown
3.
Diverse problem set spanning multiple areas of mathematics
4.
Designed to be accessible and fun, not just for students who've had years of competition training

More details to follow. Watch this space.
